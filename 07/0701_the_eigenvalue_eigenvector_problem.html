7.1 The Eigenvalue/Eigenvector Problem

Eigenvalue/Eigenvector

  • Let AAA be m×nm\times nm×n matrix. Any values of λ\lmλ for which Av=λvAv = \lm vAv=λv has nontrival solutions vvv are called eigenvalues of AAA.
  • The corresponding vectors vvv are called eigenvectors of AAA. (also refered as characteristic value and characteristic vectors).

Characteristic Polynomial/Euqation

  • The polynomial det(A−λI)det(A-\lm I)det(A−λI) is the characteristic polynomial of A. The equation det(A−λI)=0det(A-\lm I)=0det(A−λI)=0 is the characteristic equation of AAA.
  • An eigenvalue of AAA is a scalar λ\lmλ that satisfies the characteristic equation. The eigenvectors of AAA corresponding to λ\lmλ are the nonzero solution of (A−λI)v=0(A-\lm I)v = 0(A−λI)v=0.

Let AAA be n×nn\times nn×n matrix, and III be identity matrix.

Av=λvAv=λIvAv−λIv=0(A−λI)v=0\begin{aligned} Av = \lm v\\ Av = \lm I v\\ Av - \lm I v = 0\\ (A-\lm I) v = 0 \end{aligned} Av=λvAv=λIvAv−λIv=0(A−λI)v=0​

By definition of eigenvalue, we need v⃗≠0⃗\vec{v} \ne \vec{0}v≠0.
A−λIA-\lm IA−λI is not invertible.
