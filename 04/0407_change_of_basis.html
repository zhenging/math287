4.7 Change of Basis

Ordered Basis
An ordered basis is a basis in which we are keeping track of the order in which the basis vectors are written.

Component Vector
Let VVV be a vector space with ordered basis B={v1,v2,v3}B = \{v_1, v_2, v_3\}B={v1​,v2​,v3​}, and let vvv be a vector in VVV, then v=c1v1+c2v2+c3v3v = c_1v_1 + c_2v_2 + c_3v_3v=c1​v1​+c2​v2​+c3​v3​ is the unique representation of vvv as a linear combination of BBB.
The component vector of vvv relative to BBB is written [c1c2c2]\begin{bmatrix}c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_2 \end{bmatrix}⎣⎡​c1​c2​c2​​⎦⎤​ or (c1,c2,c3)(c_1, c_2, c_3)(c1​,c2​,c3​).
We use the notation [v]B=(c1,c2,c3)[v]_B = (c_1, c_2, c_3)[v]B​=(c1​,c2​,c3​).

Change of basis matrix from BBB to CCC
Let V be a vector space with ordered bases B={v1,v2,v3}B = \{v_1, v_2, v_3\}B={v1​,v2​,v3​} and C={w1,w2,⋯wn}C=\{w_1, w_2, \cdots w_n\}C={w1​,w2​,⋯wn​}. The matrix

PC←B=[[v1]C,[v2]C,[v3]C]P_{C \leftarrow B} = [[v_1]_C, [v_2]_C, [v_3]_C] PC←B​=[[v1​]C​,[v2​]C​,[v3​]C​]

is called the change of basis matrix from BBB to CCC.
The matrix changed vectors writtern in terms of B to vectors written in terms of CCC.

  • [v]C=PC←B[v]B[v]_C = P_{C \leftarrow B} [v]_B[v]C​=PC←B​[v]B​
  • PB←CP_{B \leftarrow C}PB←C​ is the inverse of PC←BP_{C \leftarrow B}PC←B​.
