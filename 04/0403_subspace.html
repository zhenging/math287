Subspace

Let SSS be a nonempty subset of a vector space VVV. If SSS is itself a vector space under the same operations of addition and scalar multiplcation as used in VVV, then we say that SSS is a subspace of VVV.

Theorem 4.3.2
Let SSS be a nonempty subset of a vector space VVV. Then SSS is a subspace of VVV if and only if SSS is cloded under the operations of addition and scalar multiplcation as used in VVV.

Zero Vector Check
If a subset of SSS of a vector space VVV fails to contain the zero vector 0\bold{0}0, then it cannot form a subspace.
